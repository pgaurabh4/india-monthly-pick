# How We Pick Every Month

One stock. Best of best. No compromises.

## The 5-Stage Process

Every monthly pick runs through a strict 5-stage funnel before a single stock is selected. This is not a screener exercise. It is a structured elimination process designed to find the one company where India's structural growth story, excellent fundamentals, and an attractive current valuation all converge at once.

---

### Stage 1 — Sector Ranking

We start with 10 sectors that have genuine structural tailwinds in India. Not themes that sound good. Sectors with specific government policy backing, measurable industry CAGR estimates, and a clear reason why India specifically (not just globally) will see outsized growth.

The 10 sectors screened each month:
- Defence Electronics and Aerospace
- Electronics Manufacturing Services and Semiconductors
- Specialty Chemicals
- Renewable Energy Equipment
- Railway and Metro Infrastructure
- Capital Goods and Power Transmission
- Healthcare, Hospitals, and Diagnostics
- Fintech and Digital Payments
- Logistics and Warehousing
- Water Treatment and Infrastructure

Each sector is scored on CAGR potential, policy support depth, and structural durability. Top 5 go to Stage 2.

---

### Stage 2 — Company Universe

For each of the top 5 sectors, we find 5–8 listed Indian companies with market cap strictly between ₹2,000 Cr and ₹30,000 Cr. This is the small cap and mid cap range where the multi-bagger potential exists. Large caps are excluded. Conglomerates where the relevant division is a small part of the whole are excluded.

This gives us a working universe of 25–40 companies.

---

### Stage 3 — Financial Screen

Every company in the universe is screened on hard criteria. All data is sourced from actual filings and verified results, not estimates.

**Pass criteria (all must be met):**
- Revenue growth ≥ 15% YoY (latest full year)
- ROE ≥ 12%
- Forward PE < 60x
- No major accounting red flags

**Red flags that disqualify immediately:**
- Promoter pledge above 20%
- Debt to equity above 1.5x in a non-financial business
- Revenue declining or growing below 10%
- SEBI action, auditor qualification, or restatement in last 3 years
- Receivables above 200 days with no clear explanation
- Negative operating cash flow while reporting PAT positive

Companies that pass are ranked by PEG ratio (forward PE divided by PAT growth rate). Lower PEG = better value for growth. Top 5 survivors go to Stage 4.

---

### Stage 4 — Deep Dive

The top 5 survivors each get a full analyst-level deep dive:

- Latest earnings call commentary and management guidance
- Order book size, composition, and customer quality
- Management track record — did they deliver on past guidance?
- Upcoming 12-month catalysts (contracts, capacity, policy)
- Competitive moat — why can't this be disrupted?
- Bear case — what would make the thesis completely wrong?
- Peer valuation comparison
- Specific proof that it is NOT a value trap

Each stock is scored on conviction from 1 to 10.

---

### Stage 5 — Final Selection

The highest-conviction survivor wins. The final pick must satisfy all three conditions simultaneously:

1. **Sector aligned with India's structural growth** — not a cyclical, not a commodity, not dependent on global cycles. A structural beneficiary of something India is building over decades.

2. **Valuation is attractive relative to growth** — forward PE that is compelling when you account for the growth rate. A stock trading at 50x with 40% PAT CAGR is cheaper than a stock trading at 20x with 5% PAT CAGR.

3. **Not a value trap** — there must be a concrete reason the stock will deliver: a signed order book, a government mandate, a new facility coming online, a structural market shift. Hope is not a thesis.

---

## What This Is Not

This is not a trading call. We do not care about the next quarter. We do not attempt to call market timing.

This is a 5–10 year conviction portfolio. One stock per month. Buy it. Don't check it every week. The research is done once, properly, so you don't have to second-guess.

---

## Why Only One Pick?

Because conviction matters more than diversification at this stage. If the research process is rigorous, one excellent pick a month beats ten mediocre ones. Over 5 years that is 60 picks, each independently vetted. That is a portfolio.

If the best pick is not obvious after running the full process — if multiple stocks are genuinely tied — we wait. A month without a pick is better than a pick without conviction.
