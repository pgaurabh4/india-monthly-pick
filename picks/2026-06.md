## #1 WINNER — EMMVEE PHOTOVOLTAIC POWER LTD (NSE: EMMVEE)

**Entry: ₹330–345 | Time horizon: 5–10 years**

### The One-Line Thesis

Emmvee is India's fastest-growing vertically integrated solar manufacturer — modules and cells today, wafers by FY29 — sitting behind an ALMM mandate that locks Chinese imports out of government-tied solar projects. It is growing at 116% with 34% EBITDA margins, net cash, and trades at a 35–40% discount to listed peers.

### Why EMMVEE Won Over INA (Same Theme, Different Outcome)

Both EMMVEE and INA play the exact same ALMM solar mandate. INA is actually cheaper at ~13x PE vs EMMVEE's ~20x. So why EMMVEE?

**1. Emmvee's cell manufacturing is proven. INA's is not.**
Emmvee produced 1,520 MW of cells in FY26 — a 2.85x increase over FY25. The integration works and is generating real revenue today. INA's 4.5 GW TOPCon cell plant at Narmadapuram has not produced a single commercial cell yet. The entire INA margin expansion thesis rests on a facility that is still being commissioned. Emmvee's margin expansion is already in the P&L.

**2. Institutional ownership.**
Emmvee: Multiple mutual funds + Jefferies Buy initiation at ₹320 in January 2026. INA: Zero mutual fund holdings, 1.09% FII. This is not temporary — index inclusion has minimum market cap and liquidity requirements. Without institutional buyers, INA can go months without a genuine bid. You can get trapped.

**3. Balance sheet.**
Emmvee: Net cash, IREDA has already sanctioned ₹3,306 Cr at 7.95% for expansion. INA: ₹2 Cr of current cash. If one quarter slips during the cell ramp, working capital stress emerges immediately. D/E will peak near 1.0x during FY27 capex — on a near-zero cash base.

**The summary:** INA has higher theoretical upside if everything goes right. Emmvee has higher probability of compounding. For a 5–10 year hold, execution probability matters more than theoretical upside.

### India Growth Story

Three government mandates converge on Emmvee simultaneously:

**1. 500 GW by 2030** — India must add 30–40 GW of solar annually. It has never done this before. Every module in every government-tied project must be ALMM-certified.

**2. ALMM framework** — List-I (modules, 2024), List-II (cells, **June 2026 — live now**), List-III (wafers, coming). Chinese solar is functionally locked out of government-tied demand. Emmvee is on both active lists.

**3. PLI for integrated manufacturing** — rewards companies with the full module+cell+wafer stack. Emmvee's vertical integration roadmap is exactly what PLI incentivises.

## #2 WATCHLIST — INA (INSOLATION ENERGY LTD)

*Source: Official BSE-filed verbatim transcript, Q4 FY26 concall, May 27 2026. Management: Manish Gupta (Chairman), Vikas Jain (MD), Ravi Dusad (CFO).*

### What Management Actually Said (Verbatim)

**FY27 revenue — Manish Gupta:**
> "Whatever the growth in FY26 should be there, the revenue growth in FY27 at least should be there."
= minimum 60% growth on ₹2,146 Cr base → **≥₹3,433 Cr guided**. CFO confirmed "50-60% growth."

**FY27 EBITDA margin — Manish Gupta:**
> "EBITDA margin should increase when our cell is operational...EBITDA margins will increase from 14% maybe to 17%, 18%."

**FY28 revenue + margin — Manish Gupta:**
> "FY28 we are targeting more than ₹5,000 crores. And if the full cell line should be operational then EBITDA should be 20% plus."

**Cell plant commissioning — contradiction between MD and Chairman:**
- Vikas Jain: *"COD targeted in Q3 of FY27"* (Oct–Dec 2026)
- Manish Gupta: *"we are thinking that cell will operational in our Q4 of this financial year"* (Jan–Mar 2027)
- Full ramp: Q1 FY28 (both agreed)

### Forward PE From Management's Own Guidance

| Scenario | Revenue | PAT Margin | PAT | Market Cap | Forward PE |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY27 (mgmt-guided ≥60% growth) | ₹3,433 Cr | 9–10% | ₹309–343 Cr | ₹2,576 Cr | **7.5–8.3x** |

At 3.7–4x FY28E PE it is absurdly cheap — **if** management delivers.

### Red Flags From the Concall

1. **Previous guidance missed:** Was 250–275 MW/month production. Quietly revised down to 2 GW/year for non-DCR. Analyst called it out directly. Management's explanation (DCR additionality) is reasonable but it is still a downward revision.
2. **Debt exploded:** ₹108 Cr → ₹835 Cr in one year. Total FY27 capex ₹2,500 Cr (₹1,500 Cr cell + ₹1,000 Cr KUSUM IPP). Peak debt FY27: ~₹1,500 Cr. D/E will hit ~1.0x.
3. **FCF negative until Q3 FY28:** CFO verbatim — *"when a company is in growth phase or capex phase, generally free cash flows are not positive...in Q4 of FY27 our cell plant will be live, and in next 6-8 months you will see a positive free cash flow."*
4. **Post-ALMM Part 2 margin uncertainty:** Gupta said *"picture will be clear after 1st June"* — management genuinely didn't know what margins would look like post-mandate at call time.
5. **Commissioning date inconsistency:** MD said Q3 FY27, Chairman said Q4 FY27. Minor but signals internal mis-coordination.
6. **Near-zero cash:** ₹2 Cr cash on books at FY26 end. No buffer if a quarter slips.

### Why It Is Still #2 and Not #1

All of INA's upside is contingent on the Narmadapuram cell plant. Until it commissions and ships one full commercial quarter at 17%+ EBITDA margins, the FY28 PE of 3.7x is a projection, not a fact. EMMVEE's cell integration is already in the FY26 P&L — proven, not promised. That is the difference.

**The buy trigger:** Narmadapuram cell plant ships first commercial quarter with EBITDA margin ≥15%. At that point the re-rating begins and you are too late if you wait for full confirmation. Watch Q3 FY27 (Oct–Dec 2026) results closely.

**Upside if it works:** 3–5x from current levels over 18–24 months. Potentially the best risk/reward in the India solar space.
