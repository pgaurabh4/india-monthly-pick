### Business

KPEL builds the "everything except the turbine" for wind farms — roads, foundations, substations, transmission lines, cabling, and Balance of Plant infrastructure. Every GW of wind capacity needs ~₹500-700 Cr of BoP work, and India is adding 8-10 GW of wind annually. The company also has a growing IPP (Independent Power Producer) portfolio at 48.5 MW, an O&M portfolio of 520+ MW, and has energised 898+ MW of capacity to date.

The company is part of the KP Group, a Gujarat-based renewable energy conglomerate with a stated 10 GW group target by 2030. Listed on the NSE main board in November 2024.

### Thesis (5–10yr)

India's wind energy capacity is projected to grow from ~48 GW to 140+ GW by 2030 — that's ~90 GW of new capacity. At ₹500-700 Cr of BoP work per GW, the addressable market is ₹45,000-63,000 Cr over the next 7-8 years. KPEL's current revenue of ₹1,488 Cr is barely scratching the surface.

The company's order book of 2.26 GW provides ~2+ years of revenue visibility at current run-rates. Recent order wins include:

- 91.4 MW hybrid project from JK Paper (Gujarat)
- 40.8 MW Wind-Solar Hybrid from Enerparc
- 100 MW Wind Project from SECI
- ₹4,000 Cr MoU with Gujarat Government for 855 MW
- MoU with Inox Wind to jointly develop 2.5 GW
- MoU with Botswana Government for $4B renewable projects

The MD's tone in public communications is aggressively ambitious: *"Whatever we will achieve, will turn out to be a miniature when compared with final goal."* The WTD adds: *"All parameters are now in our favour. Let us strike the iron as much as we can."*

### Bear Case

- **42% drop from 52-week high** (₹555 → ₹324): The NSE mainboard listing in November 2024 may have created an unsustainable pop. The current correction could reflect genuine concerns or simply post-listing profit-booking. A stock that has fallen 42% demands caution — but the fundamentals have only improved since listing (FY26 PAT +72% in Q4).
- **Working capital risk**: BoP is a project business with payment cycles that can stretch, especially with state discoms. D/E at 0.86 is manageable but not trivial.
- **Single-promoter concentration**: KP Group is a Gujarat-based family enterprise. Governance risk is real in concentrated family-run businesses.
- **Wind sector cyclicality**: If government auctions slow or state-level policy changes, order book conversion could decelerate.
- **Small-cap liquidity**: ₹2,199 Cr market cap with ~₹3-5 Cr daily volume — position sizing must account for illiquidity.

**Buy trigger:** Correction to ₹500-550 (where forward PE hits 15-16x) OR FY27Q1/H1 confirmation of ₹100+ Cr quarterly normalized PAT.

**5-year picture:** Base case ~₹550-625 (flattish near-term), bull case ₹800+ if ALMM sticks and 2,000 TPD ramps smoothly.
